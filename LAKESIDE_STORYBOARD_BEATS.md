# Lakeside: The Crossing (v5) - Storyboard Beats

Each beat is one still frame. Draw what you see described. This story is quiet — the drama is in glances, posture, proximity. No action set pieces. The tension is two people deciding whether to stop being alone.

---

## Scene 1: THE ISLAND [001-004]

**1.1** - Aerial view of a small Mediterranean island. Terra-cotta rooftops, whitewash walls, a harbor of glass-still water. Fishing boats at dock. Autumn light.

**1.2** - The harbor up close. Nets drying on the dock in afternoon sun. Painted fishing boats knocking gently against each other. A cat asleep on a coiled rope.

**1.3** - Spy walking narrow stone streets, jacket over his arm. Village nearly empty. Faded cafe awning, cracked church bell tower, a grocer's with crates of bread and wine outside.

**1.4** - Spy's room. Small, white walls, dark blue shutters thrown open. Narrow bed, wooden chair. Through the window: the harbor below, gold evening light on water.

---

## Scene 2: THE WATCHER [005-009]

**2.1** - Spy on the cafe terrace at dusk. Wine glass, unreadable local paper spread before him. Relaxed posture — but his eyes have shifted.

**2.2** - The hillside above the village. Scrub, stone, late light. Somewhere up there, unseen: a presence. The frame should feel observed.

**2.3** - Close on Spy's face. The faintest smile. He knows. He sips his wine and lets it happen.

---

## Scene 3: THE ORANGE [010-015]

**3.1** - Morning. Spy descending stone harbor steps. Below: Sniper on the seawall, legs dangling over water, eating an orange. Hat pushed back, boots dusty. He looks like he's walked a long way.

**3.2** - Sniper peeling the orange with his hunting knife. Long sun-darkened fingers separating segments. Orange juice sticky at his wrist. Unhurried.

**3.3** - Spy passing Sniper without stopping. "Bushman." Sniper not looking up. "Spook." Two words, no eye contact, everything said.

**3.4** - Spy emerging from the grocer's with a paper bag. The seawall empty. A curl of orange peel drying on the stone where Sniper sat.

---

## Scene 4: THE ORBIT [016-026]

**4.1** - The cafe, mid-morning. Spy and Sniper at separate tables. The silver-haired owner bringing espresso to each without being asked.

**4.2** - Sniper leaning forward, cup in both hands. Breaking the silence. Spy behind his newspaper, cool, composed.

**4.3** - Their eyes meeting over the newspaper. Spy: "I believe in patterns." Sniper holding the look — not quite amused, not quite wary.

**4.4** - Sniper rising, adjusting his hat, walking toward the harbor. Spy watching him go — the easy purposeful way he moves through the world.

---

## Scene 5: THE JETTY [027-052]

**5.1** - Late afternoon, golden light. The stone jetty at the end of the harbor. Sniper sitting against a mooring post, reading a battered bird guide. Sun dropping, sea turning to honey.

**5.2** - Spy arriving. Gesture toward the opposite post. "May I?" Sniper shrugging without looking up.

**5.3** - Spy seated, producing his cigarette case. Smoke curling over darkening water. The case held open toward Sniper.

**5.4** - Sniper dog-earing his page — Spy wincing — and taking a cigarette.

**5.5** - The lighter. Sniper leaning in. Close on Spy's face studying him in the flame-flicker: deep lines around his eyes, chapped lip, new scratches on his forearms. He smells of soil and brine.

**5.6** - Sniper exhaling smoke toward the horizon. Talking about the shearwater — a big seabird that spends its whole life over open water, thousands of kilometers, never touching land.

**5.8** - Spy's face. "Sounds lonely." Sniper looking at him. Something shifting between them.

**5.9** - Sniper, fingers loose around the cigarette. "They mate for life. They find each other in the dark by sound."

**5.10** - Spy flicking ash into the breeze. "If you could be anywhere right now, would you be here?" Sniper turning to face him fully. "Yeah. It is."

---

## Scene 6: THE ABSENCE [053-057]

**6.1** - Spy walking the coastal trail alone. Scrub, wind, the island falling away to sea on every side. White foam on black stone below.

**6.2** - Spy in the chair by the open window, late afternoon. Sounds drifting up: chairs scraping, a dog barking, the cafe owner shouting something fond. His face: listening, outside of it all.

**6.3** - Spy at the mirror. Freshly shaved. Thumb along his jaw. More carefully than the evening requires. Tie adjusted, cuffs set, the mask considered — then he looks away.

---

## Scene 7: THE DINNER [058-070]

**7.1** - Spy eating alone at the cafe. Grilled fish, potatoes, salad, a carafe of wine. Terrace lamplight. Eating slowly, tasting everything.

**7.2** - Sniper materializing in the chair across from him. No greeting. Hat dropped on the table as if arriving late to a standing appointment.

**7.3** - The owner bringing a second plate without surprise or comment. Spy signaling for another carafe. Watching Sniper eat.

**7.4** - "You eat like a man who doesn't know when he'll get his next meal." / "You eat like a man at his own funeral."

**7.5** - Spy's face. Blink. Mouth opening, closing — and then laughter. Clear, bright, startled out of him.

**7.6** - Sniper's face in response. Softening and sharpening at the same time. As if he'd seen an animal in the wild he'd only ever read about.

**7.7** - Spy reaching for his wine. Sniper returning to his plate. "Suits you."

---

## Scene 8: THE STEP [071-093]

**8.1** - Empty terrace. The cafe owner sweeping around them pointedly. Village dark but for the corner lamp and the low white moon grazing the water.

**8.2** - Spy and Sniper walking side by side to the stone steps. Behind a shutter, someone washing dishes. Their arms nearly touching.

**8.3** - At the foot of the steps. "Good night." / "Night." Neither moving.

**8.4** - Spy's face, quieter than he intended: "Why are you here? In this village. Why now?"

**8.5** - Sniper looking at the ground, then out at the harbor — boats still as sleeping animals. When he looks back, his expression holds something Spy has never seen: an unguarded question.

**8.6** - Sniper rubbing the back of his neck. "Saw a boat leaving and..." Spy finishing: "You got on."

**8.7** - Spy on the first step, turned back. From this slight elevation, eye to eye. Starting to say something — Sniper cutting him off: "I know."

**8.8** - Sniper smiling, just barely, under the brim of his hat. "Sleep well, spook."

**8.9** - Spy standing on the step. Sniper's footsteps on the cobblestone growing faint, then silent. Salt and dried rosemary on the breeze. The cracked bell tolling midnight.

---

## Scene 9: THE ROOM [094-099]

**9.1** - Spy's small white room. Sitting on the edge of the bed. Tie loosened. The solitude he came here for, pressing in differently now.

**9.2** - Spy removing the mask. The silence not peaceful but expectant — walls waiting for a knock that won't come.

**9.3** - Jacket hung on the bedpost. Spy lying back on the narrow bed, feet on the floor, shoes still on. Harbor lights painting shifting shapes on the ceiling.

**9.4** - Close on Spy's face, eyes open. Thinking of the shearwater — that solitary bird flying for years over empty ocean.

**9.5** - Spy's hand pressed to his chest. Feeling the absurd knocking persist. The bird who nonetheless returns to the same shore, the same nest, the same voice calling out across the dark.

## Notes for Storyboard Artist

- **Proximity is the story.** Separate tables, arms nearly touching, eye to eye on a step. Track the closing distance frame by frame.

- **Light does the work.** Autumn Mediterranean — honey, gold, terra-cotta warmth. The jetty scene transitions from gold to dark. The final room is lit only by harbor reflections on the ceiling.

- **The mask** appears twice: Scene 6 (mirror, adjusting it) and Scene 9 (removing it alone). It should feel like armor in the first and surrender in the second.

- **Sniper's physicality** — dusty boots, knife-peeled orange, sticky wrist, dog-eared page, soil and brine smell. He is body and earth. Spy is fabric and control. The contrast should be visible in every shared frame.

- **The orange peel** (3.4) is the first object left behind. A trace. It matters.

- **Scene 5 is the hinge.** The jetty conversation carries the most weight. The lighter moment (5.5) is the closest they've been physically — faces inches apart. Hold that.

- **The laugh** (7.5) is the turn. Spy has been composed for the entire story. This breaks it. Sniper's reaction (7.6) tells us he knows what just happened.

- **The ending is interior.** No resolution, no knock at the door. Just a man alone with his hand on his chest, thinking about a bird that flies alone but always comes home.
