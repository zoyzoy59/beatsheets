## Notes for Storyboard Artist

- **The dog** appears throughout as an anchor to ordinary life. Same scrawny stray, always present in domestic scenes, always refusing to enter the swamp.

- **The transfer mechanic** (Beth strong/Ursula weak, then reversing) should be visible in body language, posture, energy. Key moments: 10.5 (Beth running, Ursula slow), 11.8 (transfer at night), 13.12 (Beth glowing, Ursula wrecked).

- **The creature** is mostly unseen. Suggest it through water movement, light, Ursula's face changing. When it appears in visions/dreams, it should feel vast and formless rather than clearly defined.

- **The mob** should include recognizable faces from the refugee caravan (6.5) — the same people, now broken and looking for someone to blame. The woman who yanked the boy's arm leads them.

- **Ursula's transformation** is gradual. Her face becomes "wrong" over Act Two — too still, too dark, something ancient flickering behind her eyes. By the confrontation (21.2), she's clearly changed.

- **Scene 10 contrast** is crucial: the undertaker's kindness lands while we see Ursula hollow and Beth thriving. External kindness, internal cost — same frame.

- **The ending** is deliberately ordinary. Same gestures as the opening, but everything is different now.
